\section{Mixed-Language Example}

The bilingual nature of production systems --- particularly those targeting
Hebrew-language document generation --- highlights a deeper engineering truth:
\textbf{language is a first-class concern in agent design}.
Prompt templates, tool schemas, and output validators must all be aware of script
direction, character encoding (\texttt{UTF-8}), and BiDi rendering rules.

The following sentence demonstrates inline mixing --- an English sentence containing
embedded Hebrew technical terms rendered correctly under \texttt{polyglossia}:

\begin{hebrew}
הסוכן מבצע \foreignlanguage{english}{\emph{ReAct loop}} —
לולאת \foreignlanguage{english}{Reason-Act} שבה הסוכן מנתח,
בוחר כלי (\foreignlanguage{english}{\emph{tool}}),
מבצע, ומשנה את מצבו בהתאם לתצפית
(\foreignlanguage{english}{\emph{observation}}).
\end{hebrew}

This bilingual capability is critical for Israeli academic submissions, where citations,
technical terminology, and abstracts often mix Hebrew and
English~\parencite{Segal2026}.

\section{Token Economics of Bilingual Processing}

Hebrew text incurs higher tokenisation overhead than English because
\texttt{BPE}-based tokenisers generate shorter sub-tokens for non-Latin scripts.
Let $\tau_{\mathrm{he}}$ be the average tokens per Hebrew word and
$\tau_{\mathrm{en}}$ the average tokens per English word.
For a bilingual document of $W_{\mathrm{he}}$ Hebrew words and $W_{\mathrm{en}}$
English words, the total token count $T$ is

\begin{equation}
  T = \tau_{\mathrm{he}}\,W_{\mathrm{he}} + \tau_{\mathrm{en}}\,W_{\mathrm{en}}
  \label{eq:bidi_tokens}
\end{equation}

Empirically, $\tau_{\mathrm{he}} \approx 2.4$ and $\tau_{\mathrm{en}} \approx 1.3$
for \texttt{claude-3} tokenisers \parencite{Segal2026}.
The \emph{bilingual overhead ratio} $\rho$ measures how much more expensive a
fully-Hebrew document is compared to an equivalent English document of the same
word count $W$:

\begin{equation}
  \rho = \frac{\tau_{\mathrm{he}}\,W}{\tau_{\mathrm{en}}\,W}
       = \frac{\tau_{\mathrm{he}}}{\tau_{\mathrm{en}}}
       \approx \frac{2.4}{1.3} \approx 1.85
  \label{eq:bidi_ratio}
\end{equation}

This ratio implies that an agent pipeline producing a 15-page Hebrew article consumes
approximately 85\% more tokens than an equivalent English pipeline, and must be
accounted for in token-budget configuration files~\parencite{Segal2026}.
